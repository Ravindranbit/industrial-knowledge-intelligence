1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Liebert DS is a precision, downflow or upflow air conditioning system
designed specifically for data center and technology room environments. It
provides close control of temperature and humidity to protect sensitive
electronic equipment.

Key Features:
- Designed for 24/7/365 continuous operation
- Variable-speed EC (Electronically Commutated) fans for energy efficiency
- iCOM™ microprocessor controls for precise environmental management
- Available in air-cooled, water-cooled, and glycol-cooled configurations
- Modular design for ease of maintenance and component replacement

3. INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 Pre-Installation Checklist

  □ Verify structural floor capacity (minimum 500 kg/m² for raised floor)
  □ Confirm electrical supply matches unit nameplate requirements
  □ Ensure minimum clearances:
    - Front: 900 mm (36") for airflow and filter access
    - Rear:  600 mm (24") for piping connections
    - Sides: 300 mm (12") for panel removal
    - Top:   450 mm (18") minimum for upflow units
  □ Verify raised floor height is adequate (minimum 300mm / 12")
  □ Confirm condensate drain location and slope (minimum 1% grade)

3.2 Piping Installation

  CRITICAL SAFETY NOTICE: All piping work must be performed by licensed
  HVAC/R technicians in compliance with local building codes.

  - Use ACR (Air Conditioning and Refrigeration) grade copper tubing only
  - All joints must be brazed under dry nitrogen purge
  - Pressure test at 450 PSI (high side) and 150 PSI (low side) for 24 hours
  - Evacuate to 500 microns or less before charging
  - Insulate suction line with minimum 19mm (3/4") closed-cell insulation
  
3.3 Condensate Drain

  - Connect 19mm (3/4") PVC condensate drain to building drain system
  - Maintain minimum 1% slope away from unit
  - Install P-trap with minimum 50mm (2") depth
  - Provide secondary (overflow) drain pan where code requires

4. iCOM™ CONTROLS — OPERATION
================================================================================

4.1 Control System Overview

The Liebert iCOM™ is an advanced microprocessor-based control system that
monitors and manages all cooling functions including:

  - Supply and return air temperature
  - Humidity sensing and control
  - Compressor staging and capacity modulation
  - Fan speed control (variable-speed EC fans)
  - Filter status monitoring
  - Alarm management and notification
  - Network communication (BACnet, Modbus, SNMP)

4.2 Operating Modes

  COOLING:      Primary mode — maintains setpoint via compressor operation
  HEATING:      Electric reheat coils activate when temperature drops below
                setpoint minus deadband
  HUMIDIFYING:  Infrared or canister-type humidifier activates when humidity
                falls below setpoint
  DEHUMIDIFYING: Overcooling + reheat cycle to remove excess moisture
  ECONOMIZER:   (DSE models) Free-cooling mode using outdoor air or pumped
                refrigerant when ambient conditions permit
  STANDBY:      Unit monitors conditions but does not actively cool
                (used in N+1 redundancy configurations)

4.3 Setpoint Configuration

  Default Temperature Setpoint:  72°F / 22°C
  Default Humidity Setpoint:     45% RH
  Temperature Deadband:          ±2°F / ±1°C (adjustable)
  Humidity Deadband:             ±5% RH (adjustable)
  High Temperature Alarm:        80°F / 27°C (adjustable)
  Low Temperature Alarm:         60°F / 16°C (adjustable)
  High Humidity Alarm:           60% RH (adjustable)
  Low Humidity Alarm:            30% RH (adjustable)

5. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
================================================================================

⚠ SAFETY WARNING: Disconnect ALL local and remote power supplies before
performing any maintenance. Fan-motor controls can retain electric charge
for up to 10 minutes after power is disconnected.

5.1 Monthly Maintenance

  □ Check and record supply and return air temperatures
  □ Check and record humidity levels
  □ Inspect condensate drain and P-trap (flush if necessary)
  □ Check iCOM display for active alarms or warnings
  □ Verify fan operation (listen for unusual noise/vibration)
  □ Check condenser coil (outdoor unit) for debris accumulation

5.2 Quarterly Maintenance (Every 90 Days)

  □ Inspect and clean evaporator coils
  □ Inspect and clean condenser coils (outdoor unit)
  □ Replace air filters (or when pressure drop exceeds 0.5" W.C.)
  □ Check refrigerant pressures (suction and discharge)
  □ Inspect electrical connections for tightness
  □ Lubricate fan bearings (if applicable — EC fans are sealed)
  □ Test condensate pump operation (if equipped)
  □ Verify temperature and humidity sensor calibration
  □ Check belt tension and condition (belt-drive models only)

5.3 Semi-Annual Maintenance (Every 6 Months)

  □ Full refrigerant system leak test
  □ Check compressor oil level and condition
  □ Inspect humidifier canister or IR lamps (replace if scaled)
  □ Test all safety controls and alarm setpoints
  □ Clean condensate pan thoroughly
  □ Inspect wiring and control board for signs of wear or corrosion
  □ Verify economizer operation (DSE models)

5.4 Annual Maintenance

  □ Complete refrigerant charge verification and documentation
  □ Comprehensive electrical system inspection
  □ Vibration analysis on compressors and fans
  □ Full calibration of all sensors (temperature, humidity, pressure)
  □ Infrared thermography of electrical connections
  □ Review and update iCOM firmware if new version available
  □ Update maintenance log and compliance documentation

5.5 Filter Specifications

  Filter Type:        MERV 8 (standard) / MERV 13 (high-efficiency option)
  Filter Size:        Varies by model — see unit nameplate
  Replacement Trigger: Pressure drop > 0.5" W.C. OR 90 days, whichever first
  Direction:          Arrow on filter frame must match airflow direction

6. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
================================================================================

6.1 Unit Not Cooling

  SYMPTOM: Supply air temperature rising, compressor not running
  POSSIBLE CAUSES:
    1. Compressor high-pressure lockout → Check condenser coil for blockage
    2. Compressor low-pressure lockout → Check for refrigerant leak
    3. Loss of airflow → Check fan operation, dirty filters
    4. Power phase loss → Verify all 3 phases present at disconnect
    5. iCOM control fault → Check display for alarm codes
  
  ACTION: Review alarm history on iCOM display. If ALM-004 (high pressure),
  check outdoor ambient temperature and condenser coil cleanliness first.

6.2 Unit Short-Cycling

  SYMPTOM: Compressor starts and stops frequently (more than 6x per hour)
  POSSIBLE CAUSES:
    1. Temperature deadband too narrow → Increase to ±2°F minimum
    2. Oversized unit for the load → Consider staging or standby mode
    3. Refrigerant overcharge → Verify charge per nameplate
    4. Faulty expansion valve → Check superheat readings
    
6.3 High Humidity

  SYMPTOM: Humidity exceeds 60% RH despite cooling operation
  POSSIBLE CAUSES:
    1. Outside air infiltration → Seal room envelope
    2. Humidifier stuck on → Check humidifier control circuit
    3. Insufficient reheat capacity → Verify reheat elements
    4. Excessive latent load → Consider dedicated dehumidification

6.4 Unusual Noise or Vibration

  SYMPTOM: New or increasing mechanical noise from unit
  POSSIBLE CAUSES:
    1. Fan bearing wear → Schedule bearing replacement
    2. Loose mounting hardware → Retighten all mounting bolts
    3. Compressor internal wear → Monitor vibration levels
    4. Refrigerant slugging → Check superheat and subcooling

7. COMMON FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

Based on field data from Liebert DS/DSE installations, the most common
failure modes and their typical frequencies:

7.1 Compressor Failure
  - Frequency: ~5% of units per year
  - Root Causes: Electrical failure (40%), mechanical wear (35%),
    liquid slugging (15%), oil migration (10%)
  - Warning Signs: Increasing noise, rising current draw, superheat drift
  - Prevention: Regular refrigerant checks, proper charge, clean condenser

7.2 Fan Motor Failure
  - Frequency: ~3% of units per year
  - Root Causes: Bearing wear (60%), electrical winding failure (25%),
    control board fault (15%)
  - Warning Signs: Vibration increase, airflow reduction, ALM-006 alarm
  - Prevention: Regular vibration monitoring, keep filters clean

7.3 Refrigerant Leak
  - Frequency: ~8% of units per year (minor leaks)
  - Root Causes: Vibration fatigue at brazed joints (45%), Schrader valve
    cap issues (30%), factory defect (15%), physical damage (10%)
  - Warning Signs: Gradual capacity loss, low suction pressure, icing on
    suction line, ALM-014 alarm
  - Prevention: Annual leak testing, vibration isolation, proper brazing

7.4 Control Board Failure
  - Frequency: ~2% of units per year
  - Root Causes: Power surge (50%), component aging (30%), moisture
    intrusion (20%)
  - Warning Signs: Erratic operation, display glitches, communication loss
  - Prevention: Surge protection, proper ventilation, firmware updates

7.5 Condensate Overflow
  - Frequency: ~12% of units per year (most common issue)
  - Root Causes: Clogged drain line (55%), failed condensate pump (25%),
    missing P-trap (10%), improper slope (10%)
  - Warning Signs: Water on floor around unit, ALM-010 alarm
  - Prevention: Monthly drain inspection, quarterly flush
